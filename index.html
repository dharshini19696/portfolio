About

The developer who became the person who decides what gets built.

I started in QA at Accenture, spent three years writing production code and leading a team of developers at Webnox, then moved fully into product and delivery — first as an Associate Project Manager at Dotworld, now as a Product Manager at Adloggs Technologies.

That path means I don't just write requirements. I can read a pull request, sit in a sprint planning call and know when an estimate is off, and translate what engineering says into something a stakeholder can act on. It's a rarer combination than most resumes make it look — and it's the reason cross-functional teams trust the roadmaps I build.
